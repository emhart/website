My name is Edmund Hart (but Ted most of the time) and broadly my research interest is in using computational tools to understand the natural world. I focus on how tools like simulation and agent based models can be used to understand population dynamics and persistence, and community assembly, organization and coexistence. I want to create a research program with seamless feedback between field experiments and computational models with each informing the other. Currently I am a post-doc at the University of British Columbia working with Leticia Aviles , after receiving my PhD with Nick Gotelli in May 2011. When I'm not doing science I love telemark skiing, sailing , hiking with my dog, and rock climbing.
